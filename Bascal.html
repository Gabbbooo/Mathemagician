Understanding Continuity

A function is continuous at x = a if:

1
f(a) exists
2
limx→a f(x) exists
3
limx→a f(x) = f(a)

Key Definition

1. f(a) exists
2. limx→a f(x) exists
3. limx→a f(x) = f(a)

Limits Explanation

Limits describe the behavior of a function as x approaches a certain value. They are essential for defining derivatives and understanding continuity. For example:

1
limx→2 (x² + 3x - 4) = 6
2
limx→5 (x² - 25)/(x - 5) = 10

In essence, limits allow us to predict the output of a function even at points where it may not be directly defined.
